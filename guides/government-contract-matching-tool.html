Government contract matching tool for small businesses

A good government contract matching tool should do more than list opportunities. It should help a business owner understand which opportunities may fit the business profile and which ones may not be worth pursuing.

Why keyword searching is not enough

Searching by keyword can produce too many unrelated results. A better matching process starts with the business capability profile, services offered, relevant NAICS codes, set-aside status, location, and the ability to perform the work.

What a matching tool should help answer

  • Does the opportunity match what the business actually does?
  • Does the NAICS code make sense for the business?
  • Does the set-aside or certification requirement fit?
  • Is the deadline realistic?
  • What staffing, compliance, or past performance issues may exist?

The CapGen direction

CapGen helps turn a business capability profile into a live government contract opportunity pipeline. The goal is to give small businesses a clearer starting point for opportunity review and pursuit planning.

From matching to action

The next step after finding a match is deciding whether to pursue. That means reviewing fit, risk, staffing, documents, and questions before investing time into a response.

Important: CapGen does not guarantee awards. It helps organize opportunity discovery and review around the information a business provides.
