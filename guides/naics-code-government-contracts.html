What NAICS code do you need for government contracts?

A NAICS code helps classify the type of work connected to a business or a government opportunity. For small businesses, NAICS codes are one of the first filters to understand when searching for government contracts.

Why NAICS matters

Government buyers use industry categories to describe services and products. If your business monitors the wrong codes, you may miss relevant opportunities or waste time reviewing contracts outside your lane.

Primary NAICS vs monitored NAICS

Your primary NAICS may describe your main line of business. Monitored NAICS codes can include related services your business can also deliver. A technology company, for example, may monitor multiple IT and consulting categories.

How to think about NAICS selection

  • Start with the services you actually provide.
  • Review prior work and revenue sources.
  • Look for codes tied to the contract types you want to pursue.
  • Avoid adding unrelated codes just to increase volume.
  • Verify current requirements before submitting a bid or registration update.
