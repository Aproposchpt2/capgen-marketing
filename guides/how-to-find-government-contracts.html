How to find government contracts for your business

Small businesses often start by searching random keywords. A stronger approach is to start with what the business can actually deliver, then use that capability profile to guide opportunity research.

Start with your capability profile

Before reviewing opportunities, write down your core services, service area, certifications, staffing capacity, past performance, and the type of work your business can perform without stretching beyond its ability.

Use NAICS codes as a search lens

Government opportunities are commonly grouped by industry classification. Your NAICS codes help narrow the field so you are not reviewing unrelated notices.

Review set-aside eligibility

Some opportunities are open competition. Others may be set aside for small businesses, SDVOSB, WOSB, HUBZone, 8(a), or other categories. A matching opportunity still needs an eligibility review.

Sort by deadline and complexity

A contract due tomorrow may not be worth chasing if it requires a technical proposal, staffing plan, pricing schedule, past performance references, and compliance documentation. Deadline risk matters.

Use a bid/no-bid filter

  1. Does the NAICS code fit?
  2. Does the set-aside fit?
  3. Can the business perform the work?
  4. Is there enough time to respond?
  5. Does the business have relevant experience?
Important: CapGen does not guarantee awards or eligibility. It helps organize the opportunity review process so business owners can make better pursuit decisions.
