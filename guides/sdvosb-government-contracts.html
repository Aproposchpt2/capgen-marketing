SDVOSB government contracts: how to review opportunity fit

Service-disabled veteran-owned small businesses may find opportunities where SDVOSB status matters. But certification fit is only one part of the decision. The business still needs to evaluate the work, deadline, compliance, and ability to perform.

Start with certification status

If an opportunity is marked for SDVOSB participation, the business should verify its current certification and eligibility status before spending time on the response.

Review the full scope of work

A set-aside may look attractive, but the work may require on-site staffing, security requirements, specialized past performance, wage determinations, subcontracting limits, insurance, or other compliance items.

Use a practical pursuit checklist

  • Does the set-aside match the business certification?
  • Does the NAICS code align with services the business performs?
  • Can the business staff and manage the work?
  • Does the business have relevant past performance?
  • Is there enough time to prepare a credible response?
  • Are subcontracting or teaming rules clear?
